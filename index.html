About Me

Building the future, one algorithm at a time

Tech Enthusiast & Mentor

I'm an 8th-grade student at Pearland Junior High West (moving to Dawson High School in 9th grade) with a deep passion for software engineering, mathematics, and robotics. My journey began 3 years ago with Python, and I've expanded into competitive programming, machine learning, game development, and hardware engineering.

As a USACO Silver division competitor (one of the few middle schoolers at this level), I solve complex algorithmic problems involving prefix sums, two-pointers, binary search, graph traversal, and dynamic programming. The leap from Bronze to Silver is described as "a significant jump in difficulty" requiring "a thorough understanding of exactly when and how to apply each concept."

I'm also a Codewars 4 Kyu holder - this ranking is described as "Competent" level, requiring complex algorithmic thinking and is "on par with many practicing junior developers worldwide." I've earned 55+ points just for this rank, and it's in the upper tier of the platform's global ranking system.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions people ask me

How did you start coding?
I started learning Python about 3 years ago when I was in 5th grade. I was always interested in games and technology, so I decided to try programming. I started with basic tutorials and gradually moved to more complex projects.
What's your favorite programming language?
Python is my favorite! It's versatile and great for everything from web development to machine learning. I've also enjoyed learning Lua for Roblox game development.
How do you balance school and coding?
I make time for coding after homework and on weekends. I'm enrolled in a Gifted & Talented program which helps me develop analytical skills that apply to both academics and programming.
What advice do you have for beginners?
Start with Python! Don't be afraid to make mistakes - they're the best way to learn. Use free resources like Khan Academy, freeCodeCamp, and practice on platforms like Codewars and LeetCode.
What are your future goals?
I want to continue improving at USACO and hopefully reach Gold division. I'm also excited to learn more about AI/ML and maybe contribute to open source projects. Eventually, I'd love to intern at a tech company!
How can I follow your work?
You can check out my GitHub for code projects, my Kaggle for ML work, and follow my Roblox games! Links are in the social section below.
